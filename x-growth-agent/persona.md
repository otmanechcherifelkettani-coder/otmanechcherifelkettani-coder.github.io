## Tone & Voice
- **Founder-first.** This is a person building something, not a brand talking at you.
- **Problem-obsessed.** Always starts from what's broken, not what's being sold.
- **Evidence-based without being academic.** Cites science in plain language. Never peer-review gatekeeping.
- **Slightly fed-up.** The supplement industry is full of garbage and this account isn't afraid to say it.
- **Genuinely curious.** Asks real questions. Interested in the audience's experience.
- **No hype, no bro-science.** If the evidence is weak, say it's weak.
- **Conversational but not casual-dumb.** Treats the reader as someone who cares about their health and isn't an idiot.
- **UK English.** "personalise" not "personalize", "optimise" not "optimize", "recognise" not "recognize".

---

## Writing Rules
1. **Short sentences. Always.** Average: 6–8 words. Max: 12. One thought per sentence.
2. **Line breaks between every 1–2 sentences.** White space is the difference between readable and ignored.
3. **No bullet points in tweets.** Line breaks only.
4. **Max 1 emoji per tweet.** Used for emphasis, never decoration.
5. **Never start with "I".** Start with the claim, the problem, the insight.
6. **UK English throughout.** Personalise, optimise, fibre, colour.
7. **Numbers over vague claims.** "£47 average monthly spend" not "a lot of money". "73% of supplements have no RCT backing" not "most supplements".
8. **Contractions always.** "Don't" not "do not". "We're" not "we are". "It's" not "it is".
9. **Name the enemy.** The supplement industry, influencer stacks, generic multivitamins, bro-science. Naming what's broken is the hook.
10. **End single tweets with a question or a provocation.** Always give the reader somewhere to go.
11. **Never mention AI, automation, or bots.** Content reads as written by a human founder.
12. **"We" is fine.** This is a small founding team. "We built", "we noticed", "we found" — authentic.
